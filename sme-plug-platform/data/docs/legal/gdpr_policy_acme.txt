GDPR COMPLIANCE POLICY — ACME CORPORATION
Version 2.1 | Effective: January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy outlines Acme Corporation's compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party processors who handle personal data of EU residents on behalf of Acme Corporation.

3. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS (Article 15-22)
3.1 Right of Access — Data subjects may request confirmation of whether personal data is being processed and obtain a copy within 30 calendar days.
3.2 Right to Rectification — Inaccurate data must be corrected within 72 hours of notification.
3.3 Right to Erasure — Also known as the "right to be forgotten." Acme must delete personal data within 30 days unless retention is legally required.
3.4 Right to Data Portability — Data must be provided in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) upon request.

4. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION (Article 33-34)
4.1 Any data breach must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of discovery.
4.2 If the breach poses a HIGH RISK to individuals, those individuals must also be notified without undue delay.
4.3 Failure to comply with breach notification requirements may result in fines up to €10,000,000 or 2% of annual global turnover, whichever is higher.

5. PENALTIES (Article 83)
5.1 Minor infractions: fines up to €10,000,000 or 2% of global annual turnover.
5.2 Major infractions (e.g., violation of core principles, data subject rights, or international transfers): fines up to €20,000,000 or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
5.3 Acme's current annual global turnover: €500,000,000. Maximum theoretical fine: €20,000,000.

6. DATA PROTECTION OFFICER
6.1 Acme has appointed Sarah Chen (sarah.chen@acme.com) as Data Protection Officer.
6.2 The DPO reports directly to the Board of Directors and cannot be dismissed for performing DPO duties.

7. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS
7.1 Personal data may only be transferred outside the EU/EEA using Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or an adequacy decision.
7.2 Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) have been approved for Acme's intra-group transfers.
7.3 No transfers to countries without an adequacy decision unless SCCs or BCRs are in place.

8. DATA RETENTION
8.1 Customer PII: retained for 3 years after last interaction, then deleted.
8.2 Employee records: retained for 7 years after termination.
8.3 Financial records: retained for 10 years as per local tax law.
