=== SECTION 1: GENERAL RISK DISCLOSURES ===
Page 2

1.1 Market Risk
Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Read all scheme-related documents carefully 
before investing. Past performance does not guarantee future results. The NAV of units may go up 
or down depending on market conditions.

1.2 Credit Risk
Debt mutual funds carry credit risk. If an issuer defaults on interest or principal payments, 
the NAV of the fund may decline. Credit ratings of underlying securities are subject to change.

1.3 Liquidity Risk
In adverse market conditions, the fund may not be able to sell its holdings at fair value, 
which could impact NAV and redemption proceeds.

1.4 Regulatory Risk
Changes in tax laws, SEBI regulations, or government policies may affect the returns and 
tax treatment of mutual fund investments.

=== SECTION 2: SUITABILITY REQUIREMENTS ===
Page 8

2.1 Risk Profiling
Investment advisors must complete a risk profiling assessment for each client before making 
product recommendations. The assessment must evaluate:
- Investment horizon
- Risk tolerance
- Financial goals
- Existing portfolio composition
- Income stability

2.2 Product Suitability Matrix
- Conservative Profile: Liquid Funds, Short Duration Funds, Fixed Deposits
- Moderate Profile: Corporate Bond Funds, Balanced Advantage Funds, Large Cap Funds
- Aggressive Profile: Mid Cap Funds, Small Cap Funds, Sectoral Funds
